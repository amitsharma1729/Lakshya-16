Augmented Reality

Physical world augmented by computer-generated sensory input

Roxent Technologies 11th and 12th Sept, 2015

Augmented reality (AR) is a live, direct or indirect, view of a physical, real-world environment whose elements are augmented by computer-generated sensory input such as sound, video, graphics or GPS data.

As a result, the technology functions by enhancing oneâ€™s current perception of reality. With the help of advanced AR technology (e.g. adding computer vision and object recognition) the information about the surrounding real world of the user becomes interactive and digitally manipulable. Artificial information about the environment and its objects can be overlaid on the real world.

During the workshop students will be introduced to basics of Augmented Reality, its applications, working. Students work on the AR IDE, build modules which include interfacing camera, identifying markers, overlaying text, image, video, 2D & 3D elements on different types of markers.

CircuiTrix

Connect to Success

Can make anything using circuits? Come and prove!

This event will comprise of two rounds. In the preliminary round, the participants will be given a problem statement and will be required to design a power electronics circuit (on paper) that fulfills all the given conditions, using any element from the given list of elements.Circuits should be accompanied by waveforms, if necessary. Designs will be judged on the basis of efficiency, cost effectiveness and longevity. In the final round, the participants will have to debug a given circuit and identify the problems associated with the given connections.
